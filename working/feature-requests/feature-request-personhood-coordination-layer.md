# Feature Request: Name "personhood as a social coordination layer" + add the constraint direction

## Problem
The idea that personhood is a social coordination layer is the book's spine, but it's diffuse — present across several chapters, never nailed down as one named, load-bearing claim. Two gaps:

1. **Unnamed.** "A person is a verb" and "the egregore" get clean named statements. This idea doesn't — it's implied but never given a sentence the reader can carry.
2. **One-directional.** The book covers emergence well (others *build* you) but under-states constraint (others *bound* you). It never says personhood is the cost of being legible/predictable enough to coordinate with, or that the same coordination which creates the self also polices it.

## Where it currently lives
- Ch2 (~L138): "we think, therefore I am. The room comes first. The self precipitates out of it."
- Ch2 (~L160): Bakunin ("freedom of nothingness"), Dostoevsky ("handed back to ourselves by other people").
- Ch2 (~L164): Hofstadter, distributed self.
- Ch4 (~L282): "Personhood was never a substance sealed inside a skull. It is something other minds do to you."
- Epilogue (~L579): "self-identity ceases to exist outside the collective."

## Proposed change (sharpening, not a rewrite)
Two surgical moves, ~2 paragraphs + one named concept total:

1. **Name it.** Give "personhood is a social coordination layer" its own crisp, load-bearing statement. Best placement: late in Ch2, as the hinge that scales the self-rung into the org-rung (Ch3).

2. **Add the constraint direction.** State that the same coordination which creates the self also limits it — personhood is the price of being predictable enough to coordinate with; other people actively bound who you're allowed to be. Tie explicitly back to the status / modelling-budget material in Ch3 (~L205–221) so the link is drawn: the coordination layer both produces and polices the self.

## Constraints
- Match existing voice: long flowing sentences, plain diction, concrete, no lists/headers in prose.
- No padding. Sharpen the existing spine; don't add filler.
- Don't restructure other chapters or touch their arguments. Surgical inserts only.

## Out of scope
- Rewriting the epilogue's metaphysics.
- New chapters or sections.
