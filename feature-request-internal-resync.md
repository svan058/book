# Feature Request: The self re-synchronises internally too (not just between people)

## The missing idea
The book's whole engine is "build the model, maintain the model, re-sync when it drifts." But it only ever runs that loop *outward* — between people (Ch2), across teams (Ch3/6), with the machine (Ch4/7). It never turns the maintenance loop back *inward*.

Ch1 establishes the committee and the narrative self, but treats it as a static fact about what you *are*: "you are the running summary they produce" (L89), "you are the story your brain keeps telling about itself" (L113). It's framed as a one-time reveal, not an ongoing process.

The claim that's missing: **we are continuously re-synchronising who we are, inside one head, the same way a team re-syncs — and some voices in the committee are louder than others, which is what decides the narrative.** The self isn't just narrated once; it's maintained, drifts, and gets pulled back into line constantly, and the result is weighted — the loud committee members win the story, the quiet ones (the unease at L91) get written out even when they're right.

## Why it matters
1. It closes the symmetry. The book's power is "one engine at every size." Right now rung one is the odd one out — it's the only level where the engine is described as a *state* rather than a *running loop*. Naming the internal re-sync makes the self obey the same law as everything above it.
2. It explains drift-of-self. The book explains how teams drift and need re-syncing (L422) but never why a *person* drifts — why you're a different self on a good day vs a bad one, after good company vs bad. Answer: the committee's weighting shifts, the loud voices change, the narrative re-compiles. Same mechanism, inside.
3. It sets up Ch5 (the congruent self) properly. "Collapse the selves into one" lands harder once the reader sees the self as something continuously re-synchronised — coherence becomes an active maintenance practice, not a one-off discovery.

## Where it currently lives (and what's incomplete)
- L89: the committee → running summary. (Static. Needs: this summary is re-compiled continuously, not written once.)
- L91: "that unease is a committee member with a model you have not consciously read." (Closest existing hook — the quiet voice. Needs: extend to weighting — why the quiet voice loses.)
- L107: "the committee failing to agree" when you're torn. (Treated as an edge case. It's actually the loop made visible — same as the dream committee falling out of sync at L91.)
- L422/424: re-synchronisation named explicitly — but only for orgs. Never mirrored back to the self.

## Proposed change (sharpening, not new chapters)
1. **Name the inner loop.** Late in Ch1, state that the narrative self is not written once but continuously re-synchronised — the committee is always drifting and re-compiling, and "you" are the maintenance, not the output.
2. **Add the weighting.** Make explicit that committee voices are not equal — some are louder (habit, mood, chemistry, recent input, status anxiety) and the loud ones write the official story while the quiet ones get edited out, even when the quiet one holds the better model. Tie back to the L91 unease.
3. **Mirror it forward.** One line connecting internal re-sync to the org re-sync in Ch3/6 (L422), so the reader sees it's literally the same loop at two sizes — explicitly close the symmetry the book currently leaves open at rung one.

## Constraints
- Match voice: long flowing sentences, concrete, no lists/headers in prose.
- Sharpening only — no new sections, no restructure. Target ceiling ~2 paragraphs in Ch1 + one cross-reference line.
- Don't touch the corporate chapters' arguments; just reference them.

## Out of scope
- Reworking the four-rung ladder framing in the Overview.
- Ch5's congruent-self material (this sets it up; doesn't rewrite it).
